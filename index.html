Learning About Makeup

Makeup is something that has been gaining popularity in today's world. Today I will briefly discuss the history of makeup and answer questions on makeup, including some tips on how to look your best with makeup!

History of Makeup

Archaelogists believe that makeup originated in ancient Eygpt and Greece, about 7,000 years ago. At this time many women used ash from matches as kohl eyeliner and the juice of berries to stain their lips into what we know today as lipstick. On the more extreme side, some women use to ingest ox blood and put leeches on their face. They did this, hoping that they would achieve a more desirable and clear complexion. Obviously, there were many health risks that women were unaware of at this time, but they did it anyway in hopes of excelling with their beauty standards.

Makeup Tips+Advice

-What are some tips you can give for makeup?

1) First it is necessary to know what type of skin you have first, whether you're dry, oily, or combination.

2) Getting a good base is vital, so wash your face, moisturize, and prime.

3) Next step is eye makeup before face makeup to prevent eyeshadow fallout sticking on your face makeup.

4) Use foundation then concealer, this will help you use less concealer, save product, and not look cakey.

5) Set your face with setting powder so you don't crease (especially if you're oily, so you don't look greasy).

6) Finally, finish with setting spray to help your makeup stay on and look fresh throughout the day!

Finding Out Your Skin Type

Signs You Have Dry Skin

-How to know if you have dry skin?

1) After you wash your face, with your cleanser of choice your skin may feel dehydrated

2) When I say dry skin, it also means that your skin will feel very tight, redness may occur,

3) Your pores may appear small on your face

4) There might be a lot of flakiness, peeling, or scaling in your skin

5) Check when you notice flakiness, it may be more prominent during winter and even maybe some cracking too

6) Try doing a patch test, put a piece of disposable paper (tissue, bathroom tissue, napkin, paper towel) on yor face and gently press for a few seconds. Remove the paper and see if there is any dampness of wet residue on the paper. If not then the necessary oils for your face are not present, thus leading to the conclusion that you may have dry skin

Causes of Dry Skin

-Why do you have dry skin?

Well there is not one simple cause for dry skin, but here are some of the main ones below:

1) Cold weather can be a cause for dry skin, as stated above

2) Washing your face with harsh soaps. Sometimes people tend to over do it with washing their face, and this can be the cause of them having dry skin because they may be using a harsh soap or facial cleaner. Make sure to read the directions and recommended amount of times a product can be used a day, week, etc.

3) Lack of moisture in the top layers of skin can be a cause as well. That is why it is very important to keep the skin hydrated with some type of facial oil or mositurizer

4) Some external factors may be the cause of dry skin. These factors can range anywhere from sun damage, saunas, steam rooms, and many other heat or steam related factors

5) Extremely hot weather, especially in the summer. Yes, both summer and winter can make your dry skin flare up. Obviously the heat from the summer weather will reinforce the dryness of your skin

6) Taking long, hot showers or baths can dry your skin. Ensure skin moisturzation is at it's best and use more room temperature/cooler water when washing your face. So can frequent swimming, particularly in heavily chlorinated pools. Just ensure that you limit your amount of time in pools.

Signs You Have Oily Skin

-How to know if you have oily skin?

1) After you wash your face, with your cleanser of choice your skin may feel oil free but shortly after you may see and feel your oilness coming back

2) Your skin may feel very greasy and look shiny throughout the day and you may feel the need to be blotting your face

3) Your pores will appear to be very large, they may also be clogged very often

4) People with oily skin will tend to have a lot more blackheads and pimples on their face

5) Your oily skin may act up most during the summer time

6) If you're constantly using blotting sheets throughout the day and there is oily residue, there is a good chance you have dry skin

Causes of Oily Skin

-Why do you have oily skin?

1) Hormones and genetics are the main reason why many people have oily skin. Chances are that, oily skin runs in your family

2) Your diet. Oily or greasy foods can cause more oil to generate on your face

3) Certain medication can cause the amount of oil in your skin to become greater

4) Even stress can trigger the oilness in your skin to become more severe. When your body feels tense, it will release a hormone called cortisol. This will cause your oil levels to excelerate

5) Try to use a lighter, more natural amount of makeup. The amount of makeup you wear will affect your oil production, especially heavy makeup will make your skin's oil level increase dramatically

6) Overwashing your face is also bad. Yes it may dry your skin out but it will also remove the good oils and cause excessive dryness and irritation. This will increase symptoms of acne

Signs You Have Combination Skin

-How to know if you have Combination skin?

1) Pay attention to your T-Zone area(your forehead, nose, and chin), it may be oily. However, the rest of your face may be dry. Take a look at the image to your right to see where your t-zone is!

2)Large pores will be apparent on your face and they may seem clogged around the nose area. The clogged pores may not be as visible on your cheeks

3)After washing or cleansing your face, around your nose it will not feel as oily, however your skin especially areas not including your t-zone (cheeks) will feel tight and dry

4)If you have combination skin, you will find yourself applying a lot more moisturizer on your cheeks rather than any other place on your face, quite frequently

5)You will have breakouts and acne at the same time on your face

6)Your skin may flake more in the t-zone at times, rather than the cheeks due to hot weather

Causes of Combination Skin

Why do you have combination skin?

1)The weather affects how your skin feels, when it is hot your combination skin will act up more

2)Genetics, your parents may have combination skin!

How to help your combination skin?

Keep your skin well balanced. Use a moisturizer more on the cheeks and ensure that you blot, tone and exfoliate your t-zone every so often!

Signs You Have Sensitive Skin

-How to know if you have Sensitive skin?

1)Your skin may feel itchy, patchy, dry, or tight and may even burn or sting at times

2)Using new products might make your skin flare up

3)You may have normal to large pores on your skin

4)After cleansing, your skin may feel dry and irratated depending on what product you used

5)You might have to moisturize your skin often and that can cause your skin to become irratated

6)You may experience breaks out and develops rashes and red spots often

Causes of Sensitive Skin

Why do you have sensitive skin?

1)Stress can cause your skin to become very irratated

2)Certain perfumes, cleaning sprays, hair sprays, perfumes, cleaning sprays, hair sprays may have ingreidents that do not mix well with sensitive skin and that will cause it to flare up more

3)Look for mild products without fragrance and harsh ingredients like alcohol and menthol, these will inflame your skin and leave your face with a burning sensation

4)Avoid harsh scrubs that contain crushed walnuts, pumice or aluminum oxide crystals

5)Use lukewarm water, nothing too hot or too cold. Also, avoid scrubbing, especially with bar soaps as those tend to have harsh ingreidients that will aggravate your sensitive skin

6)Overall, treat your skin like a baby. Use not too many harsh products or hot water, read the ingredients on products, and try to use products that say on a label "for sensitive skin"
